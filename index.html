Connected Malaysia

Tracking digital access, online behaviour, and state-level readiness across Malaysia.

Malaysia is highly connected, but connection alone does not tell the full story. This visualisation follows national access trends, rural and urban differences, online search behaviour, and state-level digital readiness.

Story framework

The visual story structure

Access baseline Digital behaviour Geography Readiness comparison Conclusion Metadata

Section 01

National digital access baseline

The first section establishes Malaysia’s national ICT access position. It compares overall access indicators with the remaining urban-rural internet access gap, showing that digital access is high but not evenly experienced.

Section takeaway: Malaysia’s access baseline is strong, but deeper digital participation and rural access still require attention.

Section 02

Digital behaviour over time

After establishing Malaysia’s access baseline, this section examines what Malaysians search for online. Google Trends is used as a behavioural signal for interest in artificial intelligence, digital wallets, online banking, online shopping, and online streaming.

Section takeaway: Search behaviour reveals how digital access translates into everyday online activity across finance, shopping, entertainment, and emerging AI.

Section 03

State-level digital behaviour geography

After identifying the digital behaviours that dominate nationally, this section maps where digital behaviour appears stronger across Malaysian states and federal territories.

Section takeaway: Digital behaviour is not evenly distributed across Malaysia. Mapping state-level scores helps reveal where digital demand is stronger.

Section 04

State-level digital readiness comparison

After mapping where digital behaviour appears stronger, this section compares Malaysian states and federal territories directly. It examines behaviour patterns by state, regional score distributions, and each state's dominant digital behaviour.

Section takeaway: State and regional comparisons make geographic differences easier to interpret and show which places appear more digitally ready.

Section 05

Digital readiness profile and conclusion

The final section brings population context and time-series comparison into the analysis. It tests whether larger states also show stronger digital behaviour, then concludes by separating national digital behaviour trends into small multiples.

Section takeaway: Digital readiness is not explained by population size alone. Behaviour patterns vary by both geography and digital activity category.

Data sources and metadata

This visualisation uses publicly available and prepared datasets to explore Malaysia’s digital access, online behaviour, and state-level digital readiness between 2022 and 2025.

  • Malaysia district and state boundary GeoJSON files for geographic mapping

Visualisation tools

  • Vega-Lite for all charts and maps
  • HTML, CSS, and JavaScript for webpage structure
  • VS Code and Live Server for local development
  • Interactive tooltips used to provide state-level details

Important notes

  • Google Trends values are relative interest indices, not absolute search counts.
  • State-level digital behaviour scores are constructed from selected digital behaviour indicators.
  • Some maps use district polygons for geographic detail while colour represents state-level scores.
  • Population values are used as contextual indicators, not as direct measures of digital readiness.

Design focus

  • Five-section storytelling structure

  • Consistent blue visual theme across the dashboard
